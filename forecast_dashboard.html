Forecasting Methodology
1
Data Sources Historical monthly retail prices from ObservationData_aseazpd.xlsx (Jan 2016 – Dec 2024, 43 items, 37 states). Extended with NFPT Complete Dataset.csv (Nov 2024 – Jun 2025, 10 items) providing 6 additional months of recent actuals for matched items.
2
Pre-processing Outlier clipping using IQR × 3 per (item, state) series. Monthly national medians computed across all states.
3
Primary Model — Meta Prophet One Prophet model per food item, trained on the national monthly median price series. Configuration: multiplicative seasonality, yearly seasonality enabled, changepoint_prior_scale=0.3 (flexible trend to capture the 2022–2024 naira depreciation shock), seasonality_prior_scale=10, 80% confidence intervals. Prophet automatically detects structural breakpoints, handles harvest-cycle seasonality, and produces interpretable uncertainty bands.
4
Benchmark Model — LightGBM A single global gradient-boosted tree model trained across all 43 items simultaneously. Features: item encoding, month, year, t (linear time), lag prices (1, 2, 3, 6, 12 months), 3/6/12-month rolling means. This model captures cross-item patterns and is generally stronger at short-horizon forecasting.
Evaluation & Metrics Walk-forward evaluation using Jan–Dec 2024 as the hold-out test set (latest 12 months of Excel data). Metrics: MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) and RMSE. Target: MAPE < 15% across majority of items.
State-Level Forecasts State forecasts are derived by multiplying the national forecast by each state's historical price ratio (mean state price / mean national price). This preserves the geographic spread observed in the data without requiring 1,591 individual models.
Forecast Horizon Jan 2025 – Dec 2026 (24 months). Items with CSV extension are forecasted from Jul 2025 onward. The single largest upside risk is continued naira depreciation; downside scenario is improved domestic production and FX stabilization.
